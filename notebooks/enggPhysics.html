Engineering Physics

A few references for the Engineering Physics course (FIC-102) that I teach at SRM University-AP.

Recommended resources

Serway, R. A.; Jewett, J. W.; Peroomian, Vahé. Physics for Scientists and Engineers, Tenth edition.; Cengage: Boston, MA, USA, 2019.
This is the main textbook for this course. SRM-AP library has many copies of this well-written book. I strongly recommend reading relevant parts—or, better, all—of it.
Wonderful visualizations of physics.
Shankar, R. Fundamentals of Physics: Mechanics, Relativity, and Thermodynamics; Open Yale courses series; Yale University Press: New Haven, 2014.
Shankar, R. Fundamentals of Physics. II: Electromagnetism, Optics, and Quantum Mechanics; The Open Yale courses series; Yale University Press: New Haven, 2016.
Two wonderful books; and all lectures are available for free! The books are almost transcripts of what he is teaching in the class.

Additional readings

Balakrishnan, V. How Is a Vector Rotated? Resonance 1999, 4 (10), 61–68.
